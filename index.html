Layout Derives from Text Setting

Set Your Text to a Comfortable Measure

According to Robert Bringhurst in his book The Elements of Typographic Style:

Anything from 45 to 75 characters is widely regarded as a satisfactory length of line for a single-column page set in a serifed text face in a text size. The 66-character line (counting both letters and spaces) is widely regarded as ideal. For multiple-column work, a better average is 40 to 50 characters (p. 26).

Multi-column Line Lengths

Multi-column lines should strive for 40. Maybe 50. The goal: keep your readers comfortable. Be fussy. A grid’s adjacent columns produces such a setting; the same is true for CSS’s multi-columns property, which allows text to flow across multiple columns. (Grid constrains text to a single column and row.)

Some Important Caveats

Note that spaces are included in those lines. We call the length of a page’s lines of text its measure. Generally speaking, the longer the lines of text—that is, the greater their measure—the more generously we should apply line-height, also known as leading.

Discover Your Media Queries

When building a responsive design, always start from the smallest possible viewport that you can display. As you pull the viewport open, using something like the Responsive Design Mode in Firefox, you will begin to see places where your page layout and its elements begin to exhibit signs of stress: lines of text get too long to read comfortably. Images occupy too much space. The page loses its hierarchy and readers’ eyes are no longer properly guided through the content of the page.

It’s at those signs of stress that you need to make an adjustment to your typesetting, by increasing the size of the text, or to your layout: either by dropping in a min-width media query expressed in ems, or by cleverly specifying a CSS Grid that handles larger screens and additional space for your layout.

Set Your Text First

All of this advice depends on carefully set text. Again, mobile-first design is your best friend: get your typefaces chosen and set in a simple, single column (which is all there's really room for in the smallest mobile-scale views). You want your text size to be well established before braving the world of larger viewports, media queries, grid layout, and so on.
